What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

.

Imagine that your HTML is a box, filled with many other smaller boxes. each of these smaller boxes in an element. Without borders, padding and margins (and CSS) you would have a lot of trouble telling when one element end and the next begins.

Margins:

.

Margins define the space outside of an HTML element, essentially the space around your boxes. Rather than having your elemnts right up next to one another, margins allow you to separate your elements and space your content out.

Borders:

.

Borders go around an element. Think of them like a frame or a fence. They can be styled in many differnt ways and helps keep content contained tidily.
